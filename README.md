## 👋 About Me

I am a M.S. student in Computer Science &amp; Engineering at **Gyeongsang National University**, where I work in the **APEX Lab**. My research centers on **LiDAR perception for autonomous driving** — in particular **Moving Object Segmentation (MOS)** — with a focus on making 3D/4D models **fast, lightweight, and robust across heterogeneous sensors**. I also work on Visual SLAM and place recognition.

I enjoy taking ideas all the way from geometric/probabilistic foundations to real-time systems running on embedded hardware (e.g., NVIDIA Jetson). Outside of LiDAR, I have collaborated on AR/HCI, recommendation, and medical-imaging projects.

## 🔬 Research Interests

- **LiDAR Moving Object Segmentation (MOS)** — real-time, lightweight, range-robust, cross-sensor generalization
- **3D / 4D LiDAR Perception** — semantic segmentation, spatio-temporal representation learning
- **Visual SLAM &amp; Place Recognition** — ORB-SLAM, bag-of-words vocabularies
- **Efficient 3D Deep Learning** — model design for embedded/edge deployment
